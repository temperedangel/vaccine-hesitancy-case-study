02 · The experiment

Alsan & Eichmeyer:
Who Persuades the Most Hesitant?

RCT design: Respondents → Video Arms → Outcomes

Sample: 2,893 Black & White men, no college, unvaccinated for flu

Random video messages:

  • Doctor vs layperson
  • Race-concordant vs discordant
  • Expert acknowledges medical racism vs not

Outcomes:

  • Flu intent (pre & post), COVID intent
  • Trust indices: sender_index, signal_index, medical trust

04 · Data & features

What I Measured – and How I Built Hesitancy

Core features:

  • sender_index – trust in messenger (0–7)
  • signal_index – message strength (0–10)
  • likelyshot_post – flu intent (0–10)
  • covid_likely – COVID intent (0–10)
  • resp_age – age 25–50

Derived components:

  • Hesitancy groups: Most hesitant (0–2), Movable middle (3–6), Most accepting (7–10)
  • Composite medical trust index
Distributions of key variables with mean lines
05 · Exploratory patterns

Trust, Signal, and Intent
Are Entangled

  • signal_index ↔ likelyshot_post: r ≈ 0.73
  • sender_index ↔ signal_index: r ≈ 0.53
  • likelyshot_post ↔ covid_likely: r ≈ 0.40

Clear positive relationship between trust and flu intent

The flip side: this much correlation means coefficients need careful interpretation – signal, trust, and intent are not independent knobs.

Correlation matrix of trust, signal, and intent variables
06 · RQ1

RQ1: Modeling Who Moves
After the Video

Compared Linear Regression, Random Forest, XGBoost

Linear model performed best (R² ≈ 0.64; lowest RMSE/MAE)

Across models, signal_index dominates; trust & demographics are secondary

The simplest model wins – relationships are mostly smooth and additive

Linear regression outperforms tree-based models
07 · RQ2

RQ2: For Black Participants,
Expertise Needs Repair Work

Trust in messenger by treatment type and race

White participants: clear doctor trust premium over layperson

Black participants: doctor vs lay gap shrinks or disappears

In Black-only models:

  • Acknowledgment and concordant dummies have positive coefficients
  • Effects are modest but consistent with trust in non-discrimination
08 · RQ3

RQ3: Flu Messaging Leaks
Into COVID Attitudes

Flu and COVID intent: r ≈ 0.40 – a shared vaccine attitude

COVID intent model: R² in 0.4–0.5 range, MAE ~2 on 0–10 scale

Again, signal_index leads; messenger type & acknowledgment play smaller roles

Trust built in one vaccine conversation travels to COVID attitudes

Flu intent vs COVID intent – spillover confirmed
09 · Segments

Not One Hesitant Group,
but Four Profiles

KMeans on: sender_index, signal_index, flu & COVID intent

Elbow method suggests 3–4 clusters; used k = 4

PCA shows:

  • One clearly separated very low-trust / low-intent cluster
  • Overlapping movable and high-trust clusters

Lay messengers matter most for low-trust cluster; experts work for higher-trust profiles

PCA projection showing distinct hesitancy profiles

11 · Where this goes next

Where I'd Take This Next

1️⃣ Causal Pathways: Acknowledgment → Trust → Intent

Use causal mediation to quantify:

  • How much expert/lay, concordant, acknowledgment affect trust measures
  • How much of that trust flows into flu and COVID intent
  • Especially for Black respondents, to separate "warmth" vs "information" effects

2️⃣ Segment-Specific Message Testing

Treat the 4 clusters as personas:

  • Very low-trust / low-intent
  • Movable middle
  • High-trust / high-intent

Design and test different scripts per persona

Goal: move from single RCT → toolkit for trauma-informed, targeted messaging
